# Procurement Intelligence — Category Definition

## What Is Procurement Intelligence?

Procurement Intelligence is the systematic collection, analysis, and operationalization of data to increase the probability of winning public-sector contracts — before proposal development begins.

It is not proposal management. It is not CRM. It is not document storage.

Procurement Intelligence is an executive-layer discipline that answers the questions proposals cannot:

- Should we bid at all?
- What is our realistic probability of award?
- Who will we lose to and why?
- What gaps disqualify us before evaluators open our proposal?
- What does the government *actually* want vs. what they wrote in the solicitation?

## The Category Boundaries

| In Scope | Out of Scope |
|---|---|
| Bid/No-Bid decision intelligence | Proposal writing |
| Award probability modeling | CRM / pipeline tracking |
| Competitive landscape analysis | Contract administration |
| Capability gap identification | DCAA compliance |
| Past performance relevance scoring | HR / staffing |
| Teaming strategy optimization | Finance / billing |
| Knowledge vault (institutional memory) | ERP systems |
| Source selection emulation | Document management |

## Why This Is a Distinct Category

The $700B+ U.S. federal procurement market and trillions more in state, local, and international public procurement have produced a fragmented software landscape. Contractors use combinations of SAM.gov alerts, spreadsheets, Salesforce, SharePoint, and individual analyst intuition to make capture decisions worth millions of dollars.

No purpose-built platform has existed to serve the intelligence layer — until now.

Procurement Intelligence is to government contracting what Bloomberg Terminal is to finance: a dedicated intelligence substrate that professionals cannot work without once they have experienced it.
