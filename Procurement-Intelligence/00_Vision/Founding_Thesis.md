# Founding Thesis

## The Problem We Are Solving

Government contractors lose billions of dollars annually on proposals they should never have written — and fail to win contracts they were positioned to win, because they lacked the intelligence to execute a better capture strategy.

The root cause is not effort. It is information asymmetry.

Government evaluators know exactly what they want. Contractors guess.

The intelligence required to close that gap exists — in procurement histories, award databases, FOIA releases, past performance repositories, competitive filings, and industry day transcripts — but it is fragmented, voluminous, and impossible to synthesize manually at the speed capture requires.

## The Hypothesis

A purpose-built AI platform that functions as an intelligence layer for capture professionals can:

1. Reduce wasted proposal spend by 40–60% through better bid/no-bid decisions
2. Increase award probability by 15–30 percentage points through pre-proposal intelligence
3. Compress capture timelines from months to days
4. Institutionalize winning knowledge across staff turnover

## Why Now

Three forces converge in 2025–2026:

**AI capability inflection.** Large language models can now synthesize procurement documents, extract structured intelligence, and simulate government evaluation with fidelity that was impossible 24 months ago.

**Government data availability.** USASpending.gov, SAM.gov, and FOIA repositories now expose decades of procurement history in machine-readable form. The raw signal exists.

**Market readiness.** The government contracting market has watched AI transform adjacent industries. BD and capture professionals are actively seeking tools that move beyond basic pipeline CRM.

## The Category We Are Creating

We are not entering an existing software category. We are defining one.

Procurement Intelligence is a new category. Our goal is to be its defining company — the way Salesforce defined CRM and Bloomberg defined financial terminals.

Category creation requires more than a product. It requires:
- A shared vocabulary
- A founding intellectual framework  
- A community of practitioners who adopt and evangelize the category
- A platform that delivers value so undeniable it becomes infrastructure

This repository is the category creation playbook.

## The Bet

We believe that within 5 years, every government contractor above $10M annual revenue will have a Procurement Intelligence platform in their technology stack — the same way they have accounting software and CRM today.

The question is who defines what that platform is.
