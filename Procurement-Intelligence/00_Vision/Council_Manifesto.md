# Procurement Intelligence Council — Founding Manifesto

## Declaration

We, the founding members of the Procurement Intelligence Council, represent government contracting professionals who have decided that the status quo is no longer acceptable.

We have written proposals that should not have been written. We have watched contracts go to competitors for reasons that were knowable in advance. We have lost institutional knowledge to turnover, and rebuilt the same capture intelligence from scratch, year after year.

We are done accepting that as normal.

## What We Believe

**Intelligence precedes effort.** The quality of a proposal is irrelevant if the capture strategy is wrong. No amount of writing can overcome a bid decision made without adequate intelligence.

**Data is already there.** Every government contract that has ever been awarded is a data point. The intelligence exists — it is simply unorganized, unanalyzed, and unavailable at decision speed.

**AI changes the economics.** What previously required a team of analysts working for months can now be synthesized in hours. The barrier is not capability — it is adoption.

**Category definition is a competitive advantage.** The firms that understand Procurement Intelligence as a discipline — not just a tool — will dominate their markets for the next decade.

## Our Commitments

As founding council members, we commit to:

1. **Advance the discipline.** Contribute knowledge, frameworks, and case studies that elevate the practice of capture intelligence across the industry.

2. **Challenge conventional BD.** Publicly question practices that prioritize activity over intelligence — relationship calls without analysis, bids without probability assessment, proposals without competitive context.

3. **Share what works.** Publish win/loss patterns, framework refinements, and intelligence methodologies — with appropriate confidentiality — to accelerate collective learning.

4. **Hold the standard.** Insist on evidence-based decision-making. Reject gut-feel bid decisions. Demand that every go/no-go be supported by structured intelligence.

## Who We Are

The Procurement Intelligence Council is not a vendor community. It is a practitioner community convened around a shared belief that intelligence-driven capture is the future of government contracting.

Membership is by invitation. The criteria are simple: you must be a serious capture or BD professional who has decided to practice this discipline at a higher standard.
