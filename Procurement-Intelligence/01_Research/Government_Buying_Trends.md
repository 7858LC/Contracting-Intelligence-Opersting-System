# Government Buying Trends 2024–2026

## Macro Trends Shaping the Procurement Landscape

### 1. Accelerating Defense & National Security Spend

DOD budget requests have grown year-over-year, with particular acceleration in:
- Cyber and zero-trust architecture programs
- AI/ML and autonomous systems
- Hypersonic and counter-hypersonic capabilities
- Space domain awareness
- DIB (Defense Industrial Base) resilience

**Intelligence implication:** Firms without AI/ML past performance are increasingly disadvantaged. The intelligence window to reposition is 18–24 months before a major solicitation.

### 2. Small Business Set-Aside Expansion

Congress and OMB have consistently expanded small business procurement goals. The current federal goal is 23% of eligible dollars — many agencies exceed this.

Set-aside categories growing fastest:
- 8(a) Business Development
- HUBZone
- VOSB / SDVOSB
- WOSB / EDWOSB

**Intelligence implication:** Socioeconomic status intelligence is now a strategic variable. Teaming with correctly-qualified partners is a procurement strategy, not just a diversity initiative.

### 3. IDIQ / GWAC Vehicles Dominating Award Patterns

The shift from single-award to multi-award IDIQ vehicles continues. Vehicles like OASIS+, Polaris, SEWP VI, and CIO-SP4 are increasingly the preferred procurement mechanism for civilian and defense agencies.

**Intelligence implication:** Task order intelligence — who wins what, under which vehicle, at what price — is becoming as important as prime contract tracking. Firms not on relevant vehicles face structural disadvantage.

### 4. Increased Emphasis on Past Performance Recency and Relevance

Agencies are tightening past performance evaluation criteria, requiring demonstrated recent experience (typically 3–5 years) that is closely analogous in scope, complexity, and dollar value.

**Intelligence implication:** Past performance gap analysis — identifying gaps between a firm's portfolio and an agency's evaluation criteria — must happen at pursuit entry, not proposal kickoff.

### 5. Price-to-Win Pressure Intensifying

Competition intensity has increased across most categories as barriers to entry decline. Best-value tradeoffs increasingly penalize non-competitive pricing.

**Intelligence implication:** Competitive pricing intelligence — understanding competitor cost structures, labor rates, and wrap rates — is no longer optional for competitive pursuits.

### 6. AI Procurement Policy Evolution

EO 14110 and subsequent OMB guidance have established AI acquisition standards. Agencies are increasingly requiring AI transparency, explainability, and bias testing in AI-enabled contracts.

**Intelligence implication:** Firms developing AI capabilities must track policy evolution and position compliantly. The intelligence window for AI-related opportunities is accelerating.
