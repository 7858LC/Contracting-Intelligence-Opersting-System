# Procurement Intelligence — Market Landscape

## Current Market Structure

The government contracting software market is highly fragmented. No single vendor owns the intelligence layer. The landscape groups into four categories:

### 1. Pipeline / CRM Tools
*GovWin IQ, Salesforce GovCloud, Deltek Costpoint BD*

Strengths: opportunity tracking, contact management, pipeline visibility  
Gaps: no AI-driven analysis, no award simulation, no competitive intelligence synthesis, no bid/no-bid decision support

### 2. Opportunity Discovery
*Bloomberg Government, GovWin, FedBizOpps alerts, SAM.gov*

Strengths: broad market coverage, early solicitation alerts  
Gaps: raw data without intelligence extraction; requires significant human analysis

### 3. Market Research / OSINT
*USASpending.gov, FPDS, HigherGov, GovTribe*

Strengths: historical award data, incumbent identification, agency spend patterns  
Gaps: data presentation without synthesis; no forward-looking intelligence; no action recommendations

### 4. Proposal Tools
*Loopio, Responsive, Privia*

Strengths: RFP response workflows, content libraries, collaboration  
Gaps: downstream of capture; do not address the intelligence gap that precedes proposal development

## The White Space

No platform exists at the intersection of:
- Pre-solicitation intelligence synthesis
- AI-powered bid/no-bid decision support  
- Award probability modeling
- Source selection simulation
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Institutional knowledge management

This white space is the Procurement Intelligence category.

## Buyer Pain Points (Primary Research)

| Pain Point | Frequency | Cost Estimate |
|---|---|---|
| Bids on unwinnable opportunities | Very High | $50K–$500K per proposal |
| Loss of capture knowledge on turnover | High | $200K–$2M per departure |
| No structured bid/no-bid process | High | 30–50% wasted BD spend |
| Unable to identify capability gaps early | Medium | Lost awards worth $1M–$50M |
| No competitive context during capture | High | Lower win rates industry-wide |
| Duplicate research across pursuits | Medium | 40–60% redundant analyst effort |

## Serviceable Market

- Total U.S. government contractors: ~80,000
- Contractors with $5M+ annual revenue (primary buyer): ~18,000
- Contractors with dedicated BD/capture staff: ~8,000
- Initial addressable segment (>$10M revenue): ~5,000 firms
- Average revenue per account (target): $12,000–$60,000/year
- Initial SAM (bottom-up): $60M–$300M ARR

## International Expansion Opportunity

- UK Crown Commercial Service: ~£250B annual procurement
- EU public procurement: ~€2T annually
- World Bank / development bank contractors: global mid-market
- Canadian federal procurement: ~$22B annually

The intelligence problem is universal wherever public procurement exists.
