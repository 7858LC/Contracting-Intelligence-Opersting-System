# Council Recruitment Strategy

## Target Profile

**Primary target:** Senior BD and capture professionals with 7+ years in government contracting, at firms between $10M–$500M annual revenue, with demonstrated frustration with the status quo of capture intelligence practice.

**Secondary target:** Consultants and advisors (capture managers, proposal consultants, BD coaches) who serve the primary target segment and can amplify the category narrative.

**Exclusions:** Vendor representatives, government employees, academics without significant industry practice.

## Recruitment Channels

### Channel 1: LinkedIn Organic

Council membership is offered as the logical next step for engaged readers of the LinkedIn content series. The call to action in high-performing posts:

```
If this framing resonates — if you've been practicing this 
discipline at a higher standard and want to help define it — 
we're building the Procurement Intelligence Council.

50 founding seats. By application.

Link in comments.
```

### Channel 2: Direct Outreach

Personalized DMs to practitioners who:
- Engage substantively with LinkedIn content (comments, not just likes)
- Have written their own content on BD/capture intelligence
- Hold titles: VP Business Development, Chief Growth Officer, Director of Capture, BD Manager (senior)
- Work at firms in the $20M–$300M revenue range

**Outreach message template:**
```
[Name],

I've been reading your posts on [topic] and think you're practicing 
capture at a level most firms aren't.

We're building the Procurement Intelligence Council — 50 founding 
practitioners who are defining this as a discipline rather than just 
a function.

I think you'd be a strong fit. Would you have 20 minutes to learn more?

[Name]
```

### Channel 3: Conference Presence

Target events:
- APMP Bid & Proposal Con
- SAME Small Business Conference
- AFCEA TechNet
- PSC Annual Conference
- NDIA annual symposia

Present the PIMM framework as a talk/workshop. Offer council membership to attendees who self-identify as Level 3+ practitioners.

### Channel 4: Referral Chain

Each founding council member is asked to nominate 2–3 peers. The council's own network is the highest-quality recruitment channel.

## Recruitment Funnel

| Stage | Action | Target Conversion |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | LinkedIn content, conference talk | — |
| Interest | Application submitted | 5–8% of engaged followers |
| Qualification | 20-minute conversation | 60% of applicants |
| Onboarding | Welcome call + platform access | 90% of qualified |
| Active | First contribution posted | 70% within 60 days |

## Timeline

- **Month 1–2:** Launch LinkedIn series, begin direct outreach
- **Month 2–3:** First 20 council members confirmed
- **Month 3–4:** First council roundtable, content contributions begin
- **Month 4–6:** Full 50-member cohort confirmed
- **Month 6:** Founding cohort closes, Cohort 2 waitlist opens
