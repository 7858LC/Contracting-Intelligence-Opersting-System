## Series 1: The Failure Mode Series
*(7 posts, one per failure mode from Chapter 02)*

**Post 1 — The Relationship Mirage**
```
Here is a thing contractors tell themselves that costs them contracts:

"We have a great relationship with the program office."

Relationships are necessary. They are not sufficient.

In FAR Part 15 best-value competitions, technical evaluation differences 
explain 68% of award outcomes.

Relationship signals explain less than 12%.

The contractor with the better technical score wins.
Not the contractor with more golf rounds with the COR.

Stop telling yourself relationships are your competitive advantage.
Start building the technical position that evaluators can actually credit.
```

## Series 2: The Intelligence Audit Series
*(5 posts, each surfacing one intelligence question firms cannot answer)*

```
Question for government contractors:

What is your win rate by agency?

Not overall. By agency.

Most firms cannot answer this.

If you cannot answer it, you cannot know whether your BD investment 
in Agency X is generating returns or burning capital.

Procurement Intelligence starts with measuring what matters.

What does your win rate data tell you?
```

## Series 3: The Maturity Model Series
*(5 posts, one per maturity level)*

```
Level 1 Procurement Intelligence:

"We bid what comes across our desk."

SAM.gov alerts. Existing client calls. Leadership gut feel.

This describes approximately 45% of government contractors.

Typical win rate at Level 1: 8–15%.

The same firms at Level 3 win at 30–42%.
At Level 4: 42–58%.

The difference is not effort. It is intelligence.

Which level are you?
```
