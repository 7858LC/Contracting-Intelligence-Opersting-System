# Go-To-Market Outreach Strategy

## GTM Philosophy

We are not selling software. We are creating a category.

The GTM strategy is sequenced accordingly:
1. **Define the category** (this repository, the State of Procurement Intelligence report)
2. **Build the community** (Procurement Intelligence Council)
3. **Publish the evidence** (LinkedIn series, frameworks, research)
4. **Convert the believers** (council members become early customers)
5. **Scale through proof** (case studies → inbound → sales motion)

Most SaaS companies invert this sequence and wonder why CAC is so high.

## Phase 1: Category Seeding (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** 500 LinkedIn followers who engage regularly with Procurement Intelligence content.

**Activities:**
- Launch LinkedIn series (3 posts/week)
- Publish State of Procurement Intelligence 2026 report as downloadable PDF
- Begin council recruitment (target: 20 founding members confirmed)
- Personal outreach to 50 targeted practitioners

**Success metrics:**
- 500+ LinkedIn followers
- 20+ council applications received
- 10+ substantive comments per post (avg)

## Phase 2: Community Activation (Months 3–6)

**Goal:** 50 active council members, 200 pilot platform users.

**Activities:**
- Council onboarding and first roundtable
- Pilot platform access to council members
- Council members publish first contributions
- Conference presence (1–2 events)
- Email newsletter launch (weekly)

**Success metrics:**
- 50 founding council members
- 200 platform trial activations
- 30% trial-to-paid conversion among council members

## Phase 3: Market Expansion (Months 6–12)

**Goal:** $500K ARR, 50 paying customers.

**Activities:**
- Paid LinkedIn advertising (targeting BD/capture titles at GovCon firms)
- Partner channel development (APMP chapters, capture consultant network)
- Case study publication (3–5 customer win stories)
- PR in FCW, GovExec, Washington Technology

**Success metrics:**
- 50 paying customers
- $500K ARR
- NPS > 50

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Company:** Government contractor, $10M–$500M annual revenue, 3+ BD/capture staff  
**Buyer:** VP Business Development, Chief Growth Officer, Director of Capture  
**Pain:** Losing winnable contracts, wasting proposal budget, losing institutional knowledge to turnover  
**Trigger events:** Recent loss of major recompete, new BD leader hired, firm trying to break into new agency/category  

## Sales Motion

Phase 1–2: Founder-led, relationship-based  
Phase 3+: Product-led growth (free trial → conversion) + inside sales for $25K+ ACV accounts

**Pricing targets:**
- Starter: $499/month ($5,988/year)
- Professional: $1,499/month ($17,988/year)
- Enterprise: Custom ($36,000–$120,000/year)
