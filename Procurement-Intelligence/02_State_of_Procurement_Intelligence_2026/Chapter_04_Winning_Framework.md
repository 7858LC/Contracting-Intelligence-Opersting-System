# Chapter 04 — The Winning Framework

## The Intelligence-First Capture Model

The standard Shipley capture process — while valuable — is fundamentally proposal-centric. It assumes the go/no-go decision has been made and organizes activity around producing a compliant, compelling proposal.

The Intelligence-First Capture Model reorganizes capture around the intelligence decisions that precede and shape everything downstream.

## The Three Intelligence Gates

### Gate 1 — Pursuit Entry (12–24 months pre-solicitation)
**Question: Is this opportunity worth pursuing?**

Required intelligence:
- Agency budget trajectory and priority signal
- Incumbent position assessment
- Competitive field identification
- Firm fit score against anticipated requirements
- Resource availability for capture

**Decision output:** Pursue / Watch / Pass — with explicit rationale documented

**Common failure:** Skipping Gate 1 entirely. Most firms enter pursuits reactively, at RFP release, with no pre-solicitation intelligence.

---

### Gate 2 — Capture Commitment (6–12 months pre-solicitation)
**Question: Can we win, and at what cost?**

Required intelligence:
- Detailed agency priority analysis (budget justifications, congressional testimony, program reviews)
- Incumbent weakness identification (CPARS, FOIA, past performance patterns)
- Competitive counter-strategy development
- Preliminary price-to-win modeling
- Capability gap identification and remediation plan

**Decision output:** Commit / Modify / Exit — with capture investment budget approved

---

### Gate 3 — Proposal Authorization (at RFP release)
**Question: Do we bid, and at what threshold?**

Required intelligence:
- Award probability estimate (evidence-based, not intuition)
- Price-to-win range
- Evaluation criteria interpretation
- Source selection simulation results
- Final go/no-go recommendation with confidence score

**Decision output:** Bid / No-Bid — with documented rationale for post-award analysis

## The Winning Framework Principles

**Principle 1: Intelligence precedes investment.**  
No capture resources are committed until Gate 1 intelligence is assembled. This eliminates the majority of wasted proposal spend.

**Principle 2: Every decision is documented.**  
Every go/no-go decision records the intelligence basis, the assumptions made, and the expected win probability. This enables win/loss pattern analysis and organizational learning.

**Principle 3: The platform holds the memory.**  
All capture intelligence lives in a shared platform, not in individual professionals' heads. Staff turnover does not destroy institutional knowledge.

**Principle 4: Losses are mandatory investments.**  
Every debrief generates intelligence that flows back into the system — updating competitor profiles, refining agency intelligence, and improving future win probability models.

**Principle 5: Win rates are measured and managed.**  
Win rates by agency, contract type, competitive set, and BD professional are tracked and reviewed quarterly. What gets measured gets improved.
