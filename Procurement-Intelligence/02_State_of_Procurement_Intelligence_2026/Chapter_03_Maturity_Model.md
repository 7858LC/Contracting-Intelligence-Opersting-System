# Chapter 03 — Procurement Intelligence Maturity Model

See also: `03_Frameworks/Procurement_Intelligence_Maturity_Model.md` for the full framework.

## Overview

The Procurement Intelligence Maturity Model (PIMM) defines five levels of organizational capability in applying intelligence to capture decisions.

Firms at higher maturity levels win at measurably higher rates, waste less proposal spend, and build compounding intelligence advantages that are difficult for competitors to replicate.

## The Five Levels

### Level 1 — Reactive Intelligence
*Characterization: "We bid what comes across our desk."*

- Opportunity identification: SAM.gov alerts + existing client relationships
- Bid decisions: leadership intuition / revenue pressure
- Competitive analysis: none or minimal
- Past performance: tracked but not analyzed for relevance gaps
- Knowledge management: individual notes and email threads

**Estimated industry prevalence: 45%**  
**Typical win rate: 8–15%**

---

### Level 2 — Structured Intelligence
*Characterization: "We have a process, but it's manual."*

- Opportunity identification: multiple data sources, some workflow
- Bid decisions: structured scoring template (e.g., Shipley factors)
- Competitive analysis: basic incumbent and competitor research
- Past performance: relevance mapping done manually pre-proposal
- Knowledge management: shared drive or CRM with inconsistent use

**Estimated industry prevalence: 40%**  
**Typical win rate: 18–28%**

---

### Level 3 — Synthesized Intelligence
*Characterization: "We analyze before we decide."*

- Opportunity identification: automated aggregation with prioritization
- Bid decisions: evidence-based scoring with explicit assumptions
- Competitive analysis: structured competitor profiling per pursuit
- Past performance: gap analysis conducted at pursuit entry
- Knowledge management: centralized, searchable, maintained

**Estimated industry prevalence: 12%**  
**Typical win rate: 30–42%**

---

### Level 4 — Predictive Intelligence
*Characterization: "We model the outcome before we commit."*

- Opportunity identification: AI-augmented with relevance scoring
- Bid decisions: quantitative win probability modeling
- Competitive analysis: price-to-win modeling, counter-strategy development
- Past performance: automated relevance scoring against requirements
- Knowledge management: AI-assisted search and synthesis

**Estimated industry prevalence: 2.5%**  
**Typical win rate: 42–58%**

---

### Level 5 — Autonomous Intelligence
*Characterization: "The platform tells us what to do next."*

- Opportunity identification: predictive pursuit pipeline, pre-solicitation shaping
- Bid decisions: AI recommendation with human confirmation
- Competitive analysis: real-time competitive monitoring and alert
- Past performance: continuous portfolio-to-opportunity relevance mapping
- Knowledge management: self-updating institutional knowledge graph

**Estimated industry prevalence: 0.5%**  
**Typical win rate: 55–70%+**

---

## Maturity Progression Investment

| Transition | Primary Investment | Typical Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| L1 → L2 | Process design, training | 3–6 months |
| L2 → L3 | Technology platform, analyst hire | 6–12 months |
| L3 → L4 | AI platform, data integration | 12–18 months |
| L4 → L5 | AI automation, feedback loops | 18–36 months |
