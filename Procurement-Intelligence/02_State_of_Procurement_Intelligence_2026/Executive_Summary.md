# State of Procurement Intelligence 2026
## Executive Summary

---

### The Core Finding

Government contractors are making $50,000–$500,000 bid decisions using the same analytical rigor they apply to a restaurant choice.

This is not a criticism of the professionals involved. It is an indictment of the tools available to them.

The information required to make excellent bid decisions exists. It is in USASpending.gov, in GAO protest decisions, in agency budget justifications, in incumbent contract vehicles, in competitive pricing histories. But it is fragmented across dozens of sources, measured in terabytes, and impossible to synthesize manually at the speed capture demands.

The result: the average government contractor bids on 3–4x more opportunities than they should, wins at rates that are a fraction of what the underlying data would predict if properly analyzed, and repeats the cycle — consuming capture resources, demoralizeing BD teams, and starving winning pursuits of attention.

### What Changed in 2025

Artificial intelligence crossed a capability threshold in 2024–2025 that makes this problem solvable for the first time.

Large language models can now:
- Synthesize hundreds of pages of procurement documents into structured intelligence in minutes
- Extract bid/no-bid signals from data that would take a senior analyst days to review
- Simulate government source selection evaluation with measurable fidelity
- Identify competitive patterns across thousands of historical awards
- Surface capability gaps between a firm's portfolio and a specific opportunity's requirements

The technology is ready. The question is whether the industry will adopt it.

### The Maturity Gap

Our research finds that fewer than 3% of government contractors practice what we define as Level 3 or higher Procurement Intelligence Maturity — systematic, AI-augmented, evidence-based capture decision-making.

The remaining 97% operate at Level 1 (reactive, gut-feel) or Level 2 (structured but manual) — leaving enormous win-rate improvement on the table.

### The Opportunity

For contractors willing to advance their Procurement Intelligence practice, the opportunity is significant:

- **Win rate improvement:** 15–30 percentage point increase in win rates is achievable within 12–18 months
- **Proposal cost reduction:** 40–60% reduction in wasted proposal spend through better bid/no-bid discipline
- **Capture cycle compression:** 60–70% reduction in time-to-intelligence for new pursuit entries
- **Knowledge retention:** Near-elimination of institutional knowledge loss from staff turnover

### Our Recommendation

Establish a Procurement Intelligence practice. Define it. Staff it. Tool it. Measure it.

The firms that do this in 2025–2026 will hold a compounding intelligence advantage over those that wait. In a market where 10–15 percentage points of win rate improvement can double revenue, the ROI is not incremental — it is transformational.
