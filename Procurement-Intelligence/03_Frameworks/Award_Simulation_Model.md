# Award Simulation Model

*Procurement Intelligence Council — Technical Framework v1.0*

---

## What Award Simulation Is

Award Simulation is the practice of emulating government source selection evaluation against a draft proposal — before submission — to identify weaknesses, significant weaknesses, deficiencies, and improvement opportunities.

It is the closest thing to a dress rehearsal that government contractors have.

## Regulatory Foundation

U.S. federal source selection is governed by:
- **FAR 15.305** — Proposal Evaluation
- **FAR 15.308** — Source Selection Decision
- **DFARS 215.300–215.308** — Defense-specific source selection
- **DOD Source Selection Procedures (2023)** — Detailed implementation guidance

Award Simulation translates these regulatory frameworks into an evaluation instrument that can assess proposals before submission.

## The Evaluation Dimensions

### For FAR Part 15 Best-Value Procurements

| Factor | Typical Weight | Assessment Scale |
|---|---|---|
| Technical Approach | 30–50% | Outstanding / Good / Acceptable / Marginal / Unacceptable |
| Past Performance | 15–30% | Substantial / Satisfactory / Limited / Unknown Confidence |
| Management Approach | 10–20% | Outstanding / Good / Acceptable / Marginal / Unacceptable |
| Price/Cost | 20–35% | Most Competitive / Competitive / Not Competitive |
| Small Business | 5–10% (if weighted) | Exceptional / Very Good / Satisfactory / Marginal / Unsatisfactory |

### For LPTA Procurements

Simulation focuses on compliance threshold assessment:
1. Technical acceptability (binary: acceptable/unacceptable)
2. Past performance confidence (risk assessment)
3. Price competitiveness

## Simulation Outputs

A complete award simulation produces:

1. **Factor ratings** — Adjectival rating for each evaluation factor with supporting rationale
2. **Weaknesses** — Aspects that increase risk of unsuccessful performance
3. **Significant Weaknesses** — Weaknesses that appreciably increase risk; require response
4. **Deficiencies** — Material failures to meet requirements; potential grounds for elimination
5. **Strengths** — Notable positive aspects that may offset weaknesses in tradeoff analysis
6. **Red Team Commentary** — Evaluator-perspective critique of proposal strategy and presentation
7. **Award Probability** — Overall probability estimate with confidence interval
8. **Improvement Priority List** — Ranked actions with estimated score impact per action

## Running a Simulation

### Manual Process (Level 3 practice)
1. Assemble evaluation panel (3–5 people, ideally including former government evaluators)
2. Score draft proposal against solicitation evaluation criteria
3. Document ratings, weaknesses, and deficiencies
4. Present findings to proposal team with revision priorities
5. Re-run simulation on revised proposal before final submission

### AI-Augmented Process (Level 4–5 practice)
1. Upload solicitation evaluation criteria to platform
2. Upload draft proposal
3. Platform runs structured evaluation using trained AI model
4. Review AI-generated ratings, weaknesses, and improvement recommendations
5. Human review and judgment applied to AI outputs
6. Iterate with revised proposal drafts

## Quality Indicators

A well-run award simulation should:
- Identify at least 3–7 weaknesses in a competitive draft proposal
- Surface at least one significant weakness that the proposal team had not anticipated
- Produce a gate-review recommendation (Approve / Revise Before Submit / Do Not Submit)
- Be completed in time to allow at least one revision cycle before final submission
