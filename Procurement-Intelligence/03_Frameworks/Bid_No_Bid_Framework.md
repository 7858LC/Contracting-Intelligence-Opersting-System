# Bid / No-Bid Decision Framework

*Procurement Intelligence Council — Standard Framework v1.0*

---

## Purpose

This framework provides a structured, evidence-based process for making bid/no-bid decisions. It replaces intuition with documented intelligence and creates an organizational record that enables win rate improvement over time.

## The Eight Decision Dimensions

Each dimension is scored 1–10. The weighted average produces an overall Bid Score.

---

### Dimension 1: Competitive Position (Weight: 20%)

**What we are assessing:** Our realistic competitive rank relative to anticipated competitors.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Clear competitive advantage; incumbent with strong CPARS; or only qualified bidder |
| 7–8 | Strong position; competitive past performance; known favorable agency perception |
| 5–6 | Competitive but no clear advantage; 3–5 equally qualified competitors |
| 3–4 | Disadvantaged position; incumbent is strong; we lack key differentiators |
| 1–2 | Significant disadvantage; bidding for intelligence / competitive presence only |

---

### Dimension 2: Past Performance Alignment (Weight: 15%)

**What we are assessing:** Quality and relevance match between our documented past performance and anticipated evaluation criteria.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Outstanding: recent, analogous, documented past performance that precisely matches |
| 7–8 | Good: relevant past performance with minor gaps |
| 5–6 | Acceptable: past performance exists but scope, recency, or dollar value gaps |
| 3–4 | Marginal: significant gaps; teaming required to address |
| 1–2 | Unacceptable: no relevant past performance; severe risk |

---

### Dimension 3: Technical Capability (Weight: 15%)

**What we are assessing:** Our genuine technical capability to perform the work at or above the level evaluators will expect.

---

### Dimension 4: Capture Investment (Weight: 10%)

**What we are assessing:** Whether we have invested in the relationship, shaping, and pre-solicitation positioning required to be competitive.

---

### Dimension 5: Price Competitiveness (Weight: 15%)

**What we are assessing:** Whether we can price competitively while maintaining required margins.

---

### Dimension 6: Strategic Value (Weight: 10%)

**What we are assessing:** Strategic importance beyond the immediate contract (new agency entry, reference-ability, capability building, teaming relationship).

---

### Dimension 7: Resource Availability (Weight: 10%)

**What we are assessing:** Whether we have adequate BD, proposal, and technical resources to mount a competitive response without compromising other pursuits.

---

### Dimension 8: Risk Assessment (Weight: 5%)

**What we are assessing:** Execution risk, pricing risk, protest risk, and relationship risk associated with this pursuit.

---

## Decision Thresholds

| Weighted Score | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| 75–100 | **BID** — Strong position, commit resources |
| 60–74 | **BID WITH CONDITIONS** — Address identified gaps before proposal investment |
| 45–59 | **LEADERSHIP DECISION** — Marginal position; explicit strategic rationale required |
| Below 45 | **NO BID** — Expected value negative; redirect resources |

## Documentation Requirement

Every bid decision must be documented with:
1. Scores for all eight dimensions with one-sentence rationale per dimension
2. Key assumptions underlying the assessment
3. The specific intelligence gaps that, if filled, would change the decision
4. The name and role of the decision authority

Documented decisions are reviewed quarterly in win/loss analysis.
