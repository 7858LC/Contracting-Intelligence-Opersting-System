# Capture Intelligence Model

*Procurement Intelligence Council — Operational Framework v1.0*

---

## Overview

The Capture Intelligence Model defines the intelligence that must be assembled, maintained, and updated throughout the capture lifecycle for a strategic pursuit.

This is not a proposal outline. It is an intelligence collection and analysis plan — the foundation on which all subsequent capture strategy and proposal activity rests.

## Intelligence Domains

### Domain 1: Agency Intelligence

**What we need to know about the buying organization:**

- **Mission priorities** — What is the agency/program trying to accomplish in the next 3–5 years?
- **Budget trajectory** — Is funding growing, flat, or declining? What is the FYDP projection?
- **Program maturity** — New program vs. recompete vs. expansion?
- **Key decision makers** — Who are the CO, COR, PM, and technical evaluators? What do we know about their priorities and preferences?
- **Acquisition history** — What have they bought before, from whom, and at what price?
- **Stated dissatisfactions** — What has the agency complained about in congressional testimony, IG reports, or public statements?
- **Political context** — What congressional pressures, executive priorities, or interagency dynamics affect this program?

### Domain 2: Competitive Intelligence

**What we need to know about other bidders:**

- **Incumbent position** — Who holds the current contract? What is their performance record? What are their known weaknesses?
- **Anticipated competitors** — Who else is likely to bid? What are their strengths and weaknesses?
- **Teaming landscape** — Who is likely to team with whom? What teaming partnerships would be threatening?
- **Pricing intelligence** — What are competitor cost structures, wrap rates, and labor rate benchmarks?
- **Socioeconomic positioning** — Are competitors positioned to exploit set-aside status? What is our counter-strategy?

### Domain 3: Requirement Intelligence

**What we need to know about what they actually want:**

- **Stated requirements** — Draft PWS/SOW/SOO analysis; what is explicitly required?
- **Unstated requirements** — What does the agency want that they haven't written down (based on program office interactions, industry day Q&A, sources sought responses)?
- **Evaluation criteria interpretation** — How have evaluators interpreted similar language in past awards (based on debriefs, protest decisions, award announcements)?
- **Compliance traps** — What are the Section L/M requirements that commonly trip up competitors?

### Domain 4: Internal Position Intelligence

**What we need to know about ourselves:**

- **Capability alignment** — Where are we genuinely strong relative to requirements?
- **Past performance gaps** — Where are our past performance references weak, old, or insufficiently analogous?
- **Price position** — Can we price to win at acceptable margins?
- **Resource gaps** — What key personnel, certifications, or clearances do we lack?
- **Teaming needs** — What gaps must we fill through teaming vs. organic development?

## Intelligence Updates

Capture intelligence is not a one-time collection. It must be updated:
- At pursuit entry (initial assessment)
- At capture commitment gate (deep dive)
- Monthly during active capture
- At solicitation release (requirements reconciliation)
- At proposal kickoff (final competitive assessment)
- Post-award (debrief and loss analysis)
